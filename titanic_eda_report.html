Critical Insights

Primary Finding

Gender Was the Dominant Survival Factor

Female passengers survived at 74.2% vs men at 18.9% — a difference of 55 percentage points. Even 3rd-class women (50%) outlived 1st-class men (36.9%). The "women and children first" evacuation protocol was rigorously enforced, making gender the single most predictive feature in the dataset.

Socioeconomic Impact

Class Determined Access to Lifeboats

1st-class passengers survived at 63% vs 3rd-class at 24.2%. Physical cabin placement — 1st class near the top, 3rd class near the waterline — combined with evacuation priority created a 2.6× survival gap across classes.

Family Structure

Traveling Alone Reduced Survival Odds

Solo travelers had a 30.4% survival rate vs 50.6% for those with family aboard. Small families appeared to coordinate evacuations effectively — but very large families (5+ members) saw survival rates collapse back below 20%.

Age Effect

Children Had a Modest Survival Advantage

Passengers under 12 survived at 57.4%, above the overall rate of 38.4%. This supports the "children first" rule being applied — but the effect is less dramatic than gender, suggesting inconsistent enforcement across classes.

Hidden Signal

Cabin Assignment Proxies Wealth Survival

Passengers with a known cabin number (likely 1st class) survived at 66.7% vs 30.0% for those without. While 77.1% of cabin data is missing, a simple binary "cabin known" feature could serve as a powerful engineered predictor for ML models.
